01

Laughter is a Workout

Laughing 100 times is roughly equivalent to 15 minutes of exercise on a stationary bike. It boosts your immune system, lowers stress hormones, and triggers the release of endorphins (the body's natural feel-good chemicals). So, watching a comedy is technically "leg day" for your face.

02

The Rubber Chicken

The origin of the "rubber chicken" as a comedy prop is unknown, but it likely originated in the burlesque shows of the 1900s to mock the food served at dinner theater.

03

Chaplin Lost!

Charlie Chaplin once entered a "Charlie Chaplin Look-Alike Contest" in San Francisco... and came in 20th place. The judges reportedly told him he "lacked the look."

04

Dying of Laughter

It is actually possible. In 1975, a man named Alex Mitchell laughed continuously for 25 minutes while watching the show The Goodies and suffered a heart failure.

05

The Canned Laughter

The "Laugh Track" was invented in the 1950s by sound engineer Charley Douglass. He hated when live audiences laughed at the wrong moments, so he built a "keyboard" that played different laughs. The Banana Peel

Why bananas? In the late 19th century, rotting banana peels were a legitimate public hazard in New York City streets. It wasn't just a joke; it was a PSA!
