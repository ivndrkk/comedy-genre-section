A Timeline of Laughter

From Greek amphitheaters to TikTok feeds.

400 BC

The Birth of Satire

It started in Ancient Greece. Playwrights like Aristophanes used comedy not just for giggles, but as a weapon to mock politicians and philosophers. The iconic "Comedy Mask" (Thalia) was born here.

16th C.

Commedia dell'arte

Originating in Italy, this was the grandfather of improv. Masked actors played stock characters (The Fool, The Greedy Old Man) in improvised scenarios. It laid the foundation for modern slapstick.

1920s

The Silent Era

Without sound, comedy had to be physical. Charlie Chaplin and Buster Keaton turned movement into poetry. The "pie in the face" and the "banana peel slip" became universal languages.

1950s

The TV Sitcom

With the invention of television came the "Situation Comedy." I Love Lucy pioneered the 3-camera setup and live studio audiences, bringing laughter directly into living rooms.

Now

The Digital Age

Comedy is now faster and more democratic than ever. From YouTube sketches to viral memes, anyone can be a comedian. Humor has become layered, meta, and instantly shareable.
