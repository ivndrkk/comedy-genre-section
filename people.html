Charlie Chaplin

The Tramp

Charlie Chaplin

The icon of the silent era. He used mime, slapstick, and other visual comedy routines to become the most famous film star in the world.

Style: Slapstick / Mime
Masterpiece: City Lights (1931)

Robin Williams

The Energy

Robin Williams

Known for his frantic energy and improvisational skills. He could switch between hilarious voices and deep emotion in a split second.

Style: Improv / Stand-up
Masterpiece: Mrs. Doubtfire (1993)

Lucille Ball

Queen of Comedy

Lucille Ball

The first female TV superstar. Her expressive face and impeccable timing on "I Love Lucy" set the standard for every sitcom that followed.

Style: Physical / Sitcom
Masterpiece: I Love Lucy

Jim Carrey

Rubber Face

Jim Carrey

A physical comedy genius who defined the 90s. His over-the-top acting and flexible face make him a living cartoon character.

Style: Physical / Absurdist
Masterpiece: The Mask (1994)

Eddie Murphy

The Superstar

Eddie Murphy

He saved SNL in the 80s and became the biggest stand-up star in history. Known for playing multiple characters in the same movie.

Style: Stand-up / Satire
Masterpiece: Coming to America (1988)

Rowan Atkinson

Mr. Bean

Rowan Atkinson

A master of visual humor. He created a character (Mr. Bean) that is loved globally because you don't need to speak English to understand the joke.

Style: Silent / Character
Masterpiece: Mr. Bean (TV)
